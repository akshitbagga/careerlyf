What is CEED?

  • Full Form: Common Entrance Examination for Design
  • Purpose: Admission into postgraduate design programs (primarily M.Des) and Ph.D in Design
  • Conducting Body: IIT Bombay (national-level exam)
  • Accepting Institutes: IITs, IISc Bengaluru, and other design schools across India
  • Frequency: Once a year (typically January)
  • Duration: 3 hours (Part A: 1 hr, Part B: 2 hrs)

Eligibility Criteria

You can appear for CEED if you meet at least one of the following:

  • Completed a graduate degree/diploma/postgraduate degree of minimum 3 years (after 10+2)
  • Appearing for the final exam of such a program in the year you take CEED
  • Passed the GD Arts diploma program (10+5 level)

Note: No upper age limit and no restriction on number of attempts. Individual institutes may have additional admission requirements.

CEED Exam Pattern

CEED is a 3-hour exam divided into two parts. Part A is a screening stage; Part B is evaluated only if shortlisted in Part A.

Particulars Part A Part B
Mode Computer-Based Test (CBT) Pen-and-Paper Test (Offline)
Duration 1 Hour 2 Hours
Question Type Objective: NAT, MSQ, MCQ Subjective: drawing, creativity, design, writing
Evaluation Screening; only shortlisted candidates get Part B evaluated Manual evaluation; significant weightage in final score
Marks ~100 (varies by year) ~100 (varies by year)
Negative Marking Yes (MCQs and sometimes MSQs) No

Syllabus Key Areas

CEED assesses aptitude for design across screening (Part A) and aptitude (Part B).

  • Visualization and Spatial Ability
  • Analytical and Logical Reasoning
  • Environmental and Social Awareness
  • Language and Creativity
  • Observation and Design Sensitivity
  • Design Thinking and Problem Solving
  • Drawing Skills: products/people/scenes with proportion, perspective, composition
  • Creativity: innovative thinking and unique solutions
  • Communication: convey ideas clearly using text + visuals
  • Problem Identification: user/context understanding and material awareness in design

CEED Test Outline

Quick snapshot of how CEED is structured:

  • Part A (CBT): Objective screening (NAT/MSQ/MCQ), 1 hour
  • Part B (Offline): Drawing + design + writing aptitude, 2 hours
  • Evaluation Rule: Part B checked only if shortlisted in Part A
  • Next Step: Apply to institutes + face DAT/Interview where required

CEED at a Glance

  • Purpose: M.Des / Ph.D admissions
  • Conducted by: IIT Bombay
  • Duration: 3 hours
  • Part A: CBT screening (1 hr)
  • Part B: Offline aptitude (2 hrs)
  • Attempts: No limit
